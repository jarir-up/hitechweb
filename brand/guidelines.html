01 — Brand Essence

Who We Are

Hi-Tech Printers is a professional offset and digital printing house based in S.I.T.E Industrial Area, Karachi. We have been operating for 20+ years — printing for schools, corporates, pharmaceutical companies, garment exporters, retailers, and everyone in between.

We are not a new brand discovering its voice. We are an established operation that is now, deliberately, building a public presence commensurate with the quality we have always delivered privately.

The Three Words

Established. Grounded. Unfazed.

Not flashy. Not loud. The brand of a press that has been running since before most of its competitors had a Facebook page. Confident without announcing it. Karachi without performing it. We do not need to convince you — we show up, print right, deliver, and move on.

What We Are Building Toward

Two bets define our digital strategy:

  1. The Printestimation — Pakistan's first online print price calculator. Real Karachi market prices. No calls, no haggling, no Pakistan Chowk theatre. Available at hitechprinters.com.pk/calculator
  2. Brand in a Box — A complete branding package (cards, letterheads, flyers, stickers) for small Karachi businesses that want to look professional without managing multiple suppliers.

Our social media presence (@hitechprinterskhi) serves a third function: becoming the most helpful voice in the Karachi print ecosystem — answering questions, demystifying pricing, and building authority before the sale.

02 — Logo

Variants

The logo consists of the wordmark Hi-Tech Printers in a condensed sans-serif with a red arc above it. There are three production variants:

Variant Filename When to Use
Inverted — No Background
(Primary)		 hitech-logo-inverted-nobg.png All dark backgrounds — website, social media, dark print materials. This is the default.
Standard — No Background hitech-logo-nobg.png Light or white backgrounds only — invoices, letterheads, certificates.
Standard — On Background hitech-logo.png Fallback only when transparent PNG causes rendering issues.

Clear Space

Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the letter "H" in the wordmark on all four sides. Never crowd the logo against an edge or another element.

Logo Rules

✓ Do

  • Use the inverted version on all dark surfaces
  • Maintain clear space on all sides
  • Scale proportionally always
  • Place on flat, uncluttered backgrounds

✗ Do Not

  • Recolor the red arc
  • Stretch or distort the logo
  • Add drop shadows or glow effects
  • Place on busy photographic backgrounds
  • Recreate the logo in other typefaces
  • Use the rasterized JPG at small sizes

03 — Color System

The palette follows a 60-30-10 rule. Navy deep dominates every surface. Mid navy fills cards, panels, and glass layers. Maroon appears once per composition — as an accent, a signature, not a wallpaper. White is the instrument of precision.

Navy Deep #0E182A 60% — all backgrounds
Navy Mid #162035 / #1E2D47 30% — cards, glass panels
Maroon #7E0001 10% — accent, signature only
White #FFFFFF Text on dark, precision element
Charcoal #2A2A2A Text on light backgrounds
WhatsApp Green #25D366 WhatsApp CTAs only — nowhere else

Critical Color Rules

  • Maroon #7E0001 is an accent — never use as a background fill or dominant color
  • Red #CC0000 belongs only to the logo arc — do not use elsewhere in designs
  • WhatsApp green is a functional color for messaging CTAs only — it is not a brand color
  • Dark mode is the default. There is no light mode for social media. Ever.

04 — Typography

The Typeface: Nacelle

Nacelle is the single typeface used across all Hi-Tech Printers digital touchpoints. It is self-hosted and covers weights 300 through 700, including italics. No other typeface is permitted on the website, social media graphics, or brand materials.

Nacelle is a geometric sans-serif with humanist warmth — it is precise without being cold. It works at display sizes without feeling empty, and at body sizes without feeling compressed. It is the right choice for a brand that is modern but not trend-chasing.

Weight Value Usage
Light 300 Large display quotes, secondary captions
Regular 400 Body copy, descriptions, UI labels
SemiBold 600 Section headers, nav items, emphasis
Bold 700 Headline type, CTAs, post headlines

Social Media Typography Rules

  • Headlines: Nacelle Bold or SemiBold, 3–6 words maximum, ALL CAPS preferred
  • Supporting text: Nacelle Regular, one short sentence, not a paragraph
  • Never use more than 2 type sizes in a single social post
  • Never use italic for emphasis on social — use weight contrast instead
  • URLs and handles: Nacelle Regular, small, at the base — not competing with the headline

For print materials where Nacelle is unavailable

Use Montserrat (Google Fonts) as the substitution typeface. Same weight hierarchy applies. Do not use Helvetica, Arial, or system fonts as brand stand-ins.

05 — Photography & Visual Language

Hi-Tech Printers' visual language is built on process, not promotion. Every image should show the work — ink, paper, press, product — not describe or sell it.

The Heidelberg Principle

Reference: Heidelberg GmbH's own Instagram and communications. Their posts are almost entirely process: ink hitting paper, rollers in motion, close-ups of fresh print runs showing registration marks and color accuracy. No lifestyle. No staged office scenes. Just craft, shot beautifully.

This is the standard. If a photograph could belong to a stock library, it does not belong to Hi-Tech Printers.

Two Asset Categories

Category Source Use For
Process / Behind-the-press /brand-pictures/ — iPhone shots of offset press, paper stacks, press room, Epson digital unit, desk Stories, BTS content, "this is where your job runs" posts, press close-ups
Portfolio / Client work /public/images/ — professional .webp shots of finished products (brochures, books, cards, labels, diaries) for 15+ named clients Social proof posts, "Our Work" content, product category carousels, client spotlights

Photography Direction for New Shots

  • Shoot close. Fill the frame with the product or the press — no dead space
  • Natural or industrial lighting — no ring flash aesthetic
  • Dark or neutral surfaces — let the print be the subject
  • Show paper texture, ink gloss, registration precision when possible
  • Never shoot blurry, never shoot with a dirty lens
  • Portrait or square formats only for Instagram — no landscape crops

What We Do Not Use

  • Stock photography of any kind
  • Smiling people holding business cards
  • Flat lay shots with coffee cups and laptops
  • Any image that could be "anyone's" brand

06 — Instagram & Social Media

Handle: @hitechprinterskhi

Design System for Posts

Every Instagram post follows the same structural logic, regardless of the content type:

  1. One dominant element — either a headline or a photograph. Never both competing
  2. One accent — a thin maroon line, a frosted glass panel, a bold border rule. One, not many
  3. Near-black background — #0E182A or deeper. Never white or light
  4. Logo present but not dominant — small, in a corner, as a signature

Design Philosophies in Use

Philosophy What It Means for Hitech Posts
Glassmorphism Frosted, semi-transparent panels floating over dark backgrounds — used for supporting text, URLs, secondary information. Creates depth and modern precision.
Neobrutalism Bold typographic aggression — massive condensed headlines, stark contrast, intentional raw edges. Used for announcement posts (launches, offers, tools). The press doesn't whisper.
Dark Editorial Magazine-style layouts — large single message, generous negative space, one strong image. Used for portfolio showcases and process content. Think Monocle, not Scroll.

Post Content Pillars

Pillar Frequency Description
The Work 2–3x / week Finished client jobs. Portfolio shots. "Just came off the press." No client name needed unless they consent.
The Press 1x / week Behind-the-scenes. Process shots. The machines, the paper, the people. Heritage, craft, capability.
The Tool 1–2x / week Printestimation feature, calculator posts, pricing transparency content. Our core digital differentiator.
The Answer Ongoing in comments Answering printing questions in other accounts' comment sections. Building authority before the follow.
The Meme / Reactive 1x / week Current events, print industry humour, relatable B2B content. Must still look good. Never low-res.

Launch Post Order

  1. Website launch — "We're online." with URL
  2. The Printestimation — Calculator tool reveal. Pakistan-first angle.
  3. Brand in a Box — Complete branding package for local businesses.

07 — Voice & Tone

The Tapal Principle

Reference: Tapal Tea's brand communication. A Jodia Bazaar dukan from 1947 that beat multinationals not by being louder, but by being more rooted. Their social presence never shouts — it simply reminds you they were here before you were born, and will be here after.

Hitech's voice carries the same energy. We do not chase. We do not perform enthusiasm. We have been printing right for 20 years — the work speaks and we occasionally translate.

Voice Characteristics

Quality Sounds Like Does Not Sound Like
Direct "Your quote is ready." "We're excited to share that pricing info is now available!"
Confident "20 years in print. One price calculator." "We think we might be one of the only ones doing this in Pakistan?"
Dry warmth "Pakistan Chowk will still be there. Your quote won't wait." "Say goodbye to the hassle of traditional printing! 🎉"
Karachi Cadence that feels local, references that land Transliterated Roman Urdu that reads like a parody

Caption Structure

  • Line 1: The hook — short, complete thought. Could stand alone.
  • Line 2–3: One expansion. Context if necessary. No essays.
  • Line 4: CTA or handle reference. One link, one action.
  • Hashtags: 5–8 maximum, in the first comment — not cluttering the caption

Punctuation & Formatting Rules

  • No exclamation marks unless the moment genuinely earns one (rare)
  • No ellipsis for dramatic effect ("Coming soon...")
  • Em dash over parentheses — Karachi speaks in asides, not footnotes
  • Numbers as numerals always: "20 years", not "twenty years"
  • Urdu/English code-switching is encouraged when natural, never forced

09 — The Don't List

✗ Visual

  • Red as a background fill (logo arc only)
  • Light backgrounds on social media posts
  • Stock photography of any kind
  • Clip art, generic icons, decorative shapes
  • Gradients as design crutches (depth only)
  • Drop shadows on the logo
  • More than one accent element per composition
  • Low resolution images — ever

✗ Voice & Tone

  • Exclamation marks as a default
  • Forced Urdu transliteration
  • Performative enthusiasm
  • Asking for follows/likes/shares directly
  • Spelling or grammar errors in any post
  • Naming specific press model on the website
  • Overselling in captions
  • Replying to criticism with defensiveness
